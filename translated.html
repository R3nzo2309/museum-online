HOW IT BEGAN
On 19 november 1798, like the France, the government decided to create a national musea. Not only a project to encourage the love to the country but also the love to culture, art and important objects. More information

THE 'ROYAL MUSEUM'
In 1808 Lodewijk Napoleon was in power of the kingdom Holland. He moved the collections to Amsterdam, the new capitol. and not just any building but the paleis op de dam, the presently used amsterdam town hall. More information

THE ‘RIJKS MUSEUM’
with Willem I as the new king from 1813, the museum moved as the 'Rijksmuseum' together with the national picturescollection originating from Den Haag to the Trippenhuis, a 17th century townspalace on the Kloveniersburgwal. More information
